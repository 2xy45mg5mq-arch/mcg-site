HUMAN RESOURCE

STORY BY MAX COYNE-GREEN & MIA VALLET

1 / 19

SHAE is a people person. As Head of HR, she's given her life to her company. And in exchange, she's been given a family, a career path, and all the luxury perks of a booming tech sector.

But when the market crashes and rapacious investor, ELLSIE, arrives for restructuring, Shae's life is restructured in the process.

Lacking hard skills needed in the new economy, Shae learns she's not as valuable as she thought. But Ellsie disagrees. In fact, she could really use a dedicated person to deal with all the...people. So when she finds Shae at her lowest, Ellsie spots a distressed asset opportunity. And offers to invest in her. Literally.

With Ellsie's backing, Shae rises quickly...even as she descends into a dark financialized world with a terrifying understanding of human value.

SEVERANCE meets MARGIN CALL in this financial horror story about the future of work.

TOP FLOOR

1 / 15

RIPLEY meets PARASITE in a Manhattan high-rise.

MURRY opens the door. That's what he does. Six days a week in the green marble lobby of the storied uptown Pyramid House. But if you asked him, he wouldn't say he's a doorman. He'd say he's an entrepreneur working as a doorman. Waiting for the right door to open for him. He's dreaming of the top. But he's still living in his sister's basement.

SERA is happy he's down there. Their whole lives, he's been getting himself stuck in holes. Leaving her to dig him out. These days, she's trying to keep him grounded. She just wants him to be okay with who he is. But Murry's listened to enough podcasts to know he can be anyone he wants to be. And there is a man he always wanted to be...

NIK lives at the top. But unlike the other tenants at the Pyramid, the suave investor in Penthouse B always looks Murry right in the face. And he sees himself. Not just because Murry helps Nik with his diabetic dog, SNACKS. But because, besides Murry's beard, the two look strikingly similar.

So when Nik needs to leave town for work, he turns to Murry with an offer: stay over in the penthouse with Snacks. But there's a catch. In such a strict co-op, Murry will get fired if anyone sees him in the unit unattended.

He'll have to make sure everyone sees Nik.

Life upstairs is a dream come true at first. But soon, Murry discovers the man he's pretending to be isn't who he thought he was. And when Murry goes missing, Sera starts investigating the last person Murry ever mentioned: the investor in the penthouse.

Blood at the Roots

1 / 6

A neo-western spin on the classic Turkish family drama.

Hatay Province. 2013. The Yilmaz family has tended their olive trees for seven generations. But a new blight has swept the land. With her brother working at a New York hedge fund, Seda is left to manage the grove—and their ailing father. But help is coming from the east. And he's armed and dangerous.

In this neo-western spin on the classic Turkish "dizi" family drama, the unlikely partners must learn to work together to keep the trees—and themselves—alive.
